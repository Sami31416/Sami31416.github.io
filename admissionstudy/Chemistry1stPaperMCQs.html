Composition

Nessler's reagent is prepared by dissolving mercuric chloride (HgCl2) in potassium iodide (KI) solution to form K2HgI4, then adding potassium hydroxide (KOH).

First Aid Principle

For acid spills on skin, use a weak base to neutralize the acid. Sodium bicarbonate (baking soda) is ideal because:

  1. It's a weak base (pH ~8.3 for 5% solution)
  2. It doesn't generate excessive heat during neutralization
  3. It's non-toxic and safe for skin

  2. Then: Apply 5% NaHCO3 solution

Correct Answer

A) Na2C2O4·2H2O (Sodium oxalate dihydrate)

Primary Standard Definition

A primary standard is a highly pure, stable compound used to prepare standard solutions of known concentration for titrations.

Characteristics of Primary Standards

  1. High purity (≥99.95%)
  2. Stable (does not decompose easily)
  3. Non-hygroscopic (does not absorb moisture)
  4. High molecular weight (reduces weighing errors)
  5. Readily soluble
  6. Reacts completely and stoichiometrically

Common Primary Standards

  • For acid-base titrations: Sodium carbonate (Na2CO3), Potassium hydrogen phthalate (KHP)
  • For redox titrations: Sodium oxalate (Na2C2O4), Potassium dichromate (K2Cr2O7)
  • For complexometric titrations: Calcium carbonate (CaCO3)

Why Others Are Not Primary Standards

  • NaOH: Hygroscopic, absorbs CO2 from air
  • H2SO4: Concentration changes due to evaporation
  • KMnO4: Decomposes in light, contains impurities

14. If a Paul-Bunge balance beam has 100 divisions (from zero to the far left), a center zero, and a rider weight of 5 mg, what is the Rider Constant in grams?
A
0.005
B
0.0005
C
0.00005
D
0.000005
Answer Explanation (Click to expand)
▼

Correct Answer

C) 0.00005 g

Step-by-Step Calculation

Given:
Rider weight = 5 mg = 0.005 g
Number of divisions = 100 (from center to left)
Total beam divisions = 100 left + 100 right = 200 divisions total
  1. Rider Constant per division:
    Rider Constant = Rider weight ÷ Total divisions
    = 5 mg ÷ 200 = 0.025 mg per division
  2. Convert to grams:
    0.025 mg = 0.000025 g per division
  3. For the question asking about Rider Constant:
    Typically, Rider Constant refers to the value when rider is at maximum position (100 divisions)
    Rider Constant = 0.025 mg/division × 100 divisions = 2.5 mg = 0.0025 g
    However, the given answer suggests they want:
    5 mg ÷ 100 = 0.05 mg = 0.00005 g
    This assumes using only the left side divisions.

Clarification

There are two interpretations of "Rider Constant":

  1. Per division: 5 mg ÷ 200 = 0.025 mg = 0.000025 g
  2. For full scale (100 divisions): 5 mg ÷ 100 = 0.05 mg = 0.00005 g

The answer key uses the second interpretation.

Food Spoilage Factors

Oxygen promotes food spoilage through:
